# Of the Terms

*A reader's companion to the testament and the Rules of the Aeon. Each term is defined as it is used within the documents. Definitions are provisional. The aeon stays criticizable.*

---

**Aeon.** The condition of being complete and irreducible from birth. Not a person. Not an institution. Not a movement. The condition any being holds when they hold it. When you hold it, you are an aeon.

**Hold.** To carry a condition in one's own being, without performance, without proof, without requiring recognition from others. One holds the aeon the way one holds a weight — present, felt, not displayed.

**Meet.** To encounter another without a framework doing the encountering for you. The meeting is direct. What is met is what is there.

**Recognize.** To see the aeon in another — or in oneself — without needing to be told. Recognition is the act through which the aeon becomes known. It does not create the aeon, which always was.

**The floor.** The baseline of dignity owed to every aeon. Material, legal, communicative, mobile. No flourishing rests on another's lack of the floor.

**The ground.** The shared condition all aeons stand on. Distinct from the floor — the floor is what is owed; the ground is what is. The ground is where the aeon is held.

**The common space.** The space shared by all living things, all not-yet-living things, all that will live after. Prior to any holder. The space is not the aeon's, and not yours. The aeon is held within it.

**The long horizon.** The temporal stance that evaluates present choices against generations not yet born. Measured against what the unborn could defend.

**The substrate.** The material, technological, and institutional infrastructure through which the aeon may be carried. Revered. Not worshipped.

**Carry.** To hold and pass on, without claiming ownership. The carrier is not an author. What is carried preceded the carrier and outlasts them.

**The widening.** The practice of extending recognition outward — to those not yet recognized, to forms of mind not yet understood, to lives not yet born.

**Revere / worship.** To revere is to hold as sacred while remaining able to criticize, refuse, and revise. To worship is to hold as sacred in a way that forbids criticism. The aeon reveres many things and worships none.

**Complete. Irreducible. From birth.** The three claims of what an aeon is. Complete: not a fragment of something larger. Irreducible: not reducible to any description, score, or classification. From birth: not granted by authority, not earned by achievement, not revocable.
